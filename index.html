    GEO-INTELLIGENCE FOR DECISION MAKING

    About

    We Create Awesome Stuff

    We monitor how the earth changes over time. We quantify the change that is relevant to your work and provide you with the analysis you need to make an informed decision. We do this by tapping into satellites with almost daily global coverage, using powerful cloud computing tools and developing machine learning algorithms to extract the relevant data. This method is scalable, affordable and provides a historical perspective.

    Crop Health

    Satellite surveillance provides timely and detailed information to manage crop production, such as plant health, soil moisture, rainfall and ground temperature. Our system provides mid and large size farmers with data related to the health of their crop and associated information twice a month.

    Forest Identification

    Deforestation is devastating the African continent. Satellite surveillance is a powerful tool in the hands of stakeholders. We can quantify tree cover within a particular area, determine forest type, such as commercial versus natural, and monitor how the area is changing over time.

    Quantifying Crops And Yields

    Through satellite sensors, each plant has a unique spectral fingerprint and once this fingerprint is identified, large areas can be scanned using machine learning to search for this plant. This allows us to quantify the square kilometres of a specific crop in a specific area. Combining it with ground-collected data enables us to estimate yield.

    Tailored Geospatial Analysis

    We also tailor our analysis specific to your needs.For example, monitoring of urban environments for buildings/construction, tree cover, green space etc. to aid authorities in urban planning and service delivery, tools to auto-update QGIS print-ready maps at the click of a button, auto-updating web maps powered by Google Sheets that visualise data on ground, mobile data collection tools and online/offline GIS systems, facilitation of aerial LiDAR and underwater bathymetric surveys in extremely harsh environments.
