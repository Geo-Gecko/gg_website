    GG on lockdown

    Up to 4 weeks ago, 'working from home' meant holing myself up in a nice cafe with a coffee and my laptop. Stark surprise when the corona crisis landed me in home-isolation with my elderly mum ("I can't eat these vegetables"), my 3-year old ("Come mama, come, come see this!" "What?" "Cooooooome maaaaaamaaaaaaaaa") and a dog who insists on nervously prancing around me as I type. All of a sudden the office seems like a mythical oasis of calm.. Will we ever be allowed back in, please?

    Now, my Gen X parent experience of lockdown is probably hardly representative of our largely Millennial and Gen Z team. After an experiment with flexible hours and working from home over a year ago, the verdict was 'no thanks'. Some regarded the office as their sanctuary away from intrusive parents/siblings/cousins/other randomers; others got lost between day, night and other undefined timezones without the piece of driftwood that is office to cling to. So how does the GG team fare now that there is no choice?

    Our initial thoughts were 'no big deal, we're on the digital highway, we're cloud citizens!' Then it turned out that we actually all still wanted to 'see' each other and started a daily morning call. Lockdown and video calling does apparently allow for more lax approaches to physical grooming, which is refreshing in some cases, more disturbing in others.. So, is it actually working? Yes, seems so! We're working in the cloud in that way circumventing internet connectivity issues and allowing several people to work on a project at the same time. We call each other in different compositions to keep stuff on track. Productivity does not seem to suffer much for now, apart from an initial week of recalibration.

    A poll amongst my colleagues points out that what we miss most is the chatter in the office. Apparently it is essential to the GG human's wellbeing that they keep up with the latest data on who beat whom on *fill in random PlayStation game*, what disgusting/romantic/bloody TV series whom is watching now, and what the latest fashions are for introduction ceremonies. Chatter does not translate as well to (video) calls, though subtle clues can be gathered from the aforementioned laxer approaches to grooming. Another finding is that half the team are night owls whose stylse are seriously cramped by morning calls. Cats score highest as preferred companions. The largest issue seems to be keeping oneself fed.

    So what does all that translate to for a future in which we can hopefully, carefully return to the office? The obvious: we need an office cat and we need to eternally honour the ladies that will hopefully restart their bountiful supply of samosa's and plates of posho to our office. Also, the daily chatter about anything and everything is a key component of what holds us together. Questions remain though: what about our 9am start? Do office hours work?
