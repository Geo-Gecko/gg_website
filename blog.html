    Monitoring Deforestation and Afforestation in the developing word: A remote sensing approach

    As the world seeks to slow the pace of climate change, preserve wildlife, and support increasing numbers of people, trees inevitably hold a major part of the answer. However, we’re losing 9.9 million acres of forests annually in Africa which is twice the world’s average deforestation rate. For example, according to FAO, forest cover in Uganda dropped from 24% to 9% of total land area since between 1990 and 2015.

    Ug-EU Imp-Exp
